What is this about?

The purpose of this blog is to account my tenure at Dev Bootcamp. It is 19 weeks of coding challenges, agile development techniques, group projects, and soft skills.

Why coding?

"The sum of human wisdom is not contained in any one language, and no single language is capable of expressing all forms and degrees of human comprehension."

My introduction to computers was very early in life. I attribute this to being the son of an incredible mother and database administrator. The long hours she spent working meant that much of my childhood was spent playing inside the cubicles of a technology corporation. Being in such close proximity to programmers I was taught to use the now extinct MS-DOS prompt to control the inner workings of a PC when I was just eight years old.

In highschool, we left America behind us and moved to France for a two year work contract. It was here that I realized a language is more than a set of grammar rules and vocab. Its a proxy of the country's personality, history and culture. My affinity for languages took me to the Brazilian portion of the Amazon, mainland Japan, and western Europe.

I learn purely for the sake of learning. It's when I'm happiest. Whether it's a new language or Salsa dancing, I love a challenge. That's what brought me to coding - there is always more to learn.
