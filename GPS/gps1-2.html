Importance of Pair Programming

Ace Burgess & Piara Sandhu

What is pair programming?

Pair programming is working on software/coding as a two man/woman team. In pair programming there are two roles, the driver, and the navigator. Sometimes there's a senior developer and a junior developer, and at sometimes both developers are equal in experience.

Why is pair programming important?

Pair programming is important because it gives us two sets of eyes on the code. Both developers can benefit from each other's knowledge as well. This dynamic is especially important when a junior developer works with a senior developer.

Comparing Driving and Navigating

The driver writes the code and the navigator directs the driver what to type and where to go. Oftentimes, the navigator and driver switch roles. Understanding both roles is equally important as a developer.
